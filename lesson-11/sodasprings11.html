History of Soda Springs

Soda Springs sounds like it would be well known for their specialty bottled soda but that is not where the name originated. The name Soda Springs actually came from a geyser where the natural pools looked like bubbly carbonated water. This spring became more widely known and used as people began traveling the area in which it was located during the times of the Oregon trail. Many people used this spring for the washing of clothes and for drinking. What they did not know was the water was not the best for drinking because of the abundance of alkalais it contained.

Soda Springs is also home to the only known "captive geyser". This geyser was created by accident when drilling for hot water for a pool. There was so much water erupting from it that the town nearly flooded. It took them months to stop the flow of water. They were finally able to control the flow of water by adding a timer valve to it controling when water erupts. This geyser is known to be more faithful than "Old Reliable".
